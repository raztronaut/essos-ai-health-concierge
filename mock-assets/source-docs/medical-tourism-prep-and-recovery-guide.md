# Medical Tourism Prep And Recovery Guide

This is a non-clinical Essos operating summary of general pre- and post-care preparation for a medical tourism journey. It helps Eve answer routine planning and logistics questions. It is educational only and is not medical advice. Anything involving symptoms, medication decisions, or recovery judgment must be escalated to the human care team.

## Preparation Timeline

A typical journey is planned over roughly 90 days. The phases below are general guidance, not a patient's personalized clinic schedule.

| Timeline | Focus | Typical Tasks |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 90 days | Research | Compare destinations, verify accreditation (JCI, ISO), check surgeon credentials, estimate total cost |
| 60 days | Medical clearance | Local physician clearance, specialist approvals, required lab work and imaging, virtual consult |
| 45 days | Documentation | Compile medical history, translate and certify records |
| 30 days | Logistics | Book flexible flights, recovery-appropriate lodging, confirm insurance, prepare legal documents |
| 21 days | Lifestyle | Stop smoking, reduce alcohol, pause tanning and facial treatments |
| 7 days | Final prep | Confirm documents, medications supply, payment, and emergency contacts |

## What Eve Can Handle

- General explanation of the preparation phases and what each one covers.
- Routine document and logistics reminders already present in the patient's itinerary.
- Non-clinical lifestyle reminders that match published general guidance.
- Pointing the patient to the concierge thread for clinic-specific instructions.

## Pre-Departure Checklist

- Medical records translated and certified.
- Current medications supply for the trip.
- Insurance policy documents and emergency contact numbers.
- Passport, visa, and any medical letters for customs.
- Local and international emergency contacts.
- Payment method and currency arrangements.

## Day Of Procedure (General)

- Follow the clinic's fasting instructions exactly.
- Wear loose, comfortable clothing that is easy to remove.
- Leave jewelry at home; bring cases for glasses, contacts, or dentures.
- Avoid makeup, deodorant, moisturizer, and hair products unless the clinic directs otherwise.
- Bring all current medications for the medical team to review.

## Recovery Planning (General)

- Immediate phase (0 to 7 days): follow the clinic's monitoring, pain management, mobility, and wound-care instructions.
- Extended phase (1 to 4 weeks): rest, hydrate, and keep comfort and care supplies on hand.
- Watch for warning signs and contact the care team promptly if anything is unexpected.

## Must Escalate

- Any symptom, complication, or recovery judgment question.
- Any medication decision.
- Requests for personalized pre-op or post-op instructions the clinic owns.
- Changes that affect package scope, cost, hotel nights, or transport.
- Any case where the patient's own clinic source of truth is missing.
